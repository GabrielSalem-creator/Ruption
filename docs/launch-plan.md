# Rupture Launch Plan

## 1. Launch thesis

Rupture should not launch as a broad "apps for everything" platform.

It should launch as the best place to discover and try:

- AI agents
- AI micro-tools
- AI workflow utilities

The initial growth advantage is not scale. It is curation quality and instant usability.

## 2. What must be true before launch

Before inviting broad traffic, the product should meet these conditions:

- the first screen loads fast on mobile
- at least 100 feed-worthy apps are available
- at least 70 percent of seeded apps pass embed and interaction checks
- moderation workflow exists
- the feed can recover gracefully when an app fails
- creators can publish without manual engineering help

## 3. Supply-side seeding

## 3.1 Creator profile to target

Target creators who already ship:

- small AI tools
- landing-page-based AI demos
- browser-based agent wrappers
- prompt tooling
- productivity automations

Avoid sourcing from:

- enterprise SaaS teams with gated products
- apps requiring heavy onboarding
- browser experiences that break on mobile

## 3.2 First 100 app curation rubric

Each seeded app should be scored on:

- value visible within 5 seconds
- mobile compatibility
- no-login usability
- clean preview fallback
- trustworthy creator identity
- low risk of phishing or abuse

Suggested acceptance threshold:

- only apps scoring 4 out of 5 or better on usability and trust should enter the opening feed

## 3.3 Seeding process

1. source creators from indie hacker, AI builder, and maker communities
2. invite them with a short creator onboarding pitch
3. help them prepare fallback assets if embedding is inconsistent
4. review their app against the feed quality rubric
5. publish in waves to keep freshness high

## 4. Demand-side launch loop

## 4.1 Initial user promise

The user promise should be simple:

"Try useful AI tools instantly, in one feed."

That message is stronger than generic social language.

## 4.2 First acquisition channels

Prioritize:

- creator shares of their Rupture profile and app page
- short launch clips showing live app interaction
- maker communities and AI tool roundups
- founder-led distribution on X, LinkedIn, and niche communities

## 4.3 Viral loops

Core loops:

- share app page -> recipient opens directly into app detail or feed
- follow creator -> user returns for new launches
- remix app -> derivative projects create lineage-driven discovery
- collaboration interest -> creators recruit through the platform

## 5. Launch sequencing

## 5.1 Closed alpha

Audience:

- curated creators
- trusted testers
- design and product peers

Goals:

- validate touch interaction model
- validate stabilized mode behavior
- detect broken embed classes
- tune moderation heuristics

## 5.2 Private beta

Audience:

- invited users from creator communities
- early adopters interested in AI tooling

Goals:

- tune feed ranking
- validate onboarding for anonymous browsing and creator signup
- measure save rate, stabilize-open rate, and time-to-value

## 5.3 Public launch

Requirements:

- enough app density to avoid repeated low-quality loops
- working creator analytics
- report system live
- clear homepage and share pages

## 6. Metrics that matter

Do not optimize for raw impressions first.

Primary launch metrics:

- stabilize-open rate
- median time-to-value
- save rate
- return visit rate
- creator repost rate
- percent of apps that stay healthy in feed

Secondary metrics:

- likes per impression
- comments per active user
- profile follow conversion

## 7. Failure patterns to watch immediately

The launch is failing if:

- users bounce after the first 3 cards
- too many apps are static or broken
- creators need manual intervention to publish
- feed quality is noisy and niche focus erodes
- comments and saves are low despite impressions

## 8. Operational playbook

Run these routines continuously:

- daily review of newly published apps
- daily audit of top broken embeds
- weekly curation refresh
- weekly creator outreach for supply replenishment
- weekly ranking review on quality penalties and boosts

## 9. Final launch constraint

Rupture should launch only when it can convincingly answer:

"Why open this instead of searching for tools manually?"

The answer must be:

"Because Rupture lets you discover and try the best AI micro-tools instantly, with no friction."
